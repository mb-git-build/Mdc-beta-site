# Automation and Orchestration

Automation and orchestration platforms help modular infrastructure scale by reducing manual operations across deployment, monitoring, workflow, capacity management, and ongoing service coordination.

This category matters because the more distributed, modular, or fast-moving the environment becomes, the less workable manual operations become. Automation is often what lets a multi-site or phased-capacity strategy stay manageable over time.

## Why this category matters

- automation reduces operating friction as infrastructure count, density, and site complexity grow
- orchestration can improve repeatability across deployment, change control, and lifecycle workflows
- modular strategies often depend on strong automation to preserve the advantages of standardization and scale
- buyers should compare not just feature breadth but where automation actually removes human bottlenecks

## Where it fits in a modular deployment

- sits across the operating layer rather than only inside one hardware category
- connects monitoring, alerting, provisioning, workflow, and service coordination functions
- becomes more important as deployments expand from one module or site into fleets or repeated patterns
- often supports the difference between a one-off infrastructure project and a scalable operating model

## What to compare

- workflow coverage across deployment, operations, and change management
- fit with existing monitoring, controls, IT, and facilities systems
- usability, repeatability, and operator trust in automated actions
- support for multi-site, modular, or phased-capacity environments
- balance between flexibility and operational simplicity

## Typical buyer questions

- Which workflows are worth automating first in this environment?
- Does this platform actually remove operational burden or just add another layer?
- How well does it integrate with monitoring, controls, and service systems already in place?
- Is the automation model safe and explainable enough for mission-critical operations?
- Will this still help when the environment doubles or triples in complexity?
