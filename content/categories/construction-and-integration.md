# Construction and Systems Integration

Construction and systems integration partners bridge modular products and working live facilities, coordinating assembly, interfaces, sequencing, field execution, and the practical conversion of purchased systems into operating infrastructure.

This category matters because strong component choices can still fail in the field if integration is fragmented. The more modular, phased, or hybrid the deployment becomes, the more valuable disciplined integration execution becomes.

## Why this category matters

- integration quality determines whether separate power, cooling, enclosure, controls, and network layers actually work together
- field sequencing and install coordination can make or break schedule reliability
- modular systems still depend on clean site interfaces, handoffs, and execution discipline once they arrive
- buyers need partners who understand both packaged infrastructure and live mission-critical environments

## Where it fits in a modular deployment

- sits between design intent and operational reality during site execution
- connects factory-built systems with civil work, utilities, controls, and final site readiness
- becomes especially important in retrofits, phased expansions, and projects with multiple vendor packages
- supports smooth handoff into commissioning and operations-readiness work

## What to compare

- experience integrating power, cooling, controls, and modular systems in live environments
- sequencing discipline, field supervision, and schedule control
- ability to coordinate multiple vendors, trades, and phased deployment packages
- fit for retrofit, greenfield, or hybrid project conditions
- depth of issue resolution during installation and turnover

## Typical buyer questions

- Who is actually responsible for making all these systems work together on-site?
- How much live-facility experience does the integrator have?
- Can they manage phased rollout without disrupting future expansion?
- How strong are they at coordination across multiple vendors and trades?
- Where does construction end and commissioning responsibility begin?
