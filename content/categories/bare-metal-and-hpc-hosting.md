# Bare Metal and HPC Hosting

Bare metal and HPC hosting providers offer dedicated compute environments for buyers who need control, performance isolation, and faster access than a greenfield build. This category sits between pure hosting convenience and full owned-infrastructure responsibility.

It matters for teams that need serious compute quickly but still care about workload behavior, environment control, and avoiding the overhead of building an entire facility from scratch.

## Why this category matters

- bare-metal and HPC hosting can provide faster access than owned builds without forcing full multitenant abstraction
- buyers often get more performance control and workload predictability than they would from generic cloud paths
- this category is useful for teams comparing control, speed, cost structure, and operational burden
- it often competes directly with hosted GPU capacity, colocation, and modular deployment strategies

## Where it fits in a modular deployment

- can serve as a near-term capacity layer while modular infrastructure is designed or delivered
- may remain part of the final mix for workloads that need dedicated performance without facility ownership
- interacts with networking, security, operations, and workload-placement strategy
- becomes especially relevant when buyers want dedicated environments but not full site-level responsibility

## What to compare

- dedicated performance characteristics and workload isolation
- deployment speed and contract flexibility
- hardware control, upgrade path, and operational boundaries
- support for AI, HPC, or specialized performance environments
- fit relative to colocation, GPU hosting, or owned modular infrastructure

## Typical buyer questions

- How much control do we really need at the infrastructure layer?
- Is this a bridge strategy or a durable part of the operating model?
- What workloads benefit most from dedicated hosted environments versus colo or owned infrastructure?
- Where are the limits around customization, monitoring, and lifecycle control?
- How quickly can this option get us live compared with the alternatives?

## Decision scenarios

Bare metal and HPC hosting decisions sit between cloud convenience and owned infrastructure control. Buyers should compare workload isolation, hardware flexibility, support boundaries, and network fit before treating dedicated hosting as interchangeable with colo or modular capacity.

- Performance-isolated workload launch: compare dedicated hardware, storage and network topology, accelerator options, and operational boundaries against generic cloud or shared hosting.
- Bridge while modular capacity is built: evaluate contract flexibility, migration path, monitoring access, and whether workloads can move cleanly when owned infrastructure arrives.
- Specialized HPC environment: prioritize scheduler support, interconnect behavior, security posture, data movement, and support for unusual hardware or software stacks.
- Control-without-facility-ownership strategy: assess which responsibilities stay with the provider and which still require internal operations expertise.

## Deployment signals

- The workload needs dedicated performance or hardware control faster than a facility project can deliver.
- Generic cloud abstraction is too expensive, unpredictable, or restrictive for the workload profile.
- The buyer wants more control than managed GPU hosting but less responsibility than colo or owned modular infrastructure.
- Migration, monitoring, and lifecycle boundaries need to be clear before the contract is useful.
