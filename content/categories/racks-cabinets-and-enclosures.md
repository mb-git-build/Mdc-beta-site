# Racks, Cabinets, and Enclosures

Racks, cabinets, containment, and enclosure systems shape density, cooling compatibility, cable management, serviceability, and physical workflow inside modular infrastructure. This category matters because the final physical environment around equipment strongly influences how usable high-density capacity really is.

Racks are not just furniture. In dense environments they become part of the power, airflow, cooling, and maintenance system.

## Why this category matters

- enclosure design affects density, airflow, containment, and service access
- rack choices influence compatibility with rear-door cooling, direct-to-chip systems, cable strategy, and power distribution
- buyers need to compare not only dimensions and strength but operational fit in the target environment
- poor enclosure decisions can quietly constrain future density growth and maintenance efficiency

## Where it fits in a modular deployment

- sits at the interface between facility systems and live compute hardware
- links cooling, power delivery, cable management, and maintenance workflows inside modules or white space
- becomes especially important in high-density or constrained-footprint deployments
- often interacts directly with rack power, cooling strategy, and service-model planning

## What to compare

- density support, structural fit, and service access
- compatibility with cooling strategy and airflow containment approach
- cable and power-management flexibility
- modular standardization and repeatability across phases or fleets
- ease of maintenance in dense or tightly packed environments

## Typical buyer questions

- Will this enclosure support the density and cooling path we actually want?
- How much operational pain does the physical layout create later?
- Is this optimized for real serviceability or just clean specifications?
- How well does it fit modular repetition across multiple phases or sites?
- What changes if rack density rises faster than expected?
