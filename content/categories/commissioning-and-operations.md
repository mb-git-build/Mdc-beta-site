# Commissioning and Operations Readiness

Commissioning and operations-readiness partners help modular infrastructure move from installed systems to reliable working capacity with fewer surprises at turnover. This category covers the practices, teams, and vendors that verify systems perform as intended and that operations can sustain them after go-live.

This work matters because many projects are technically complete before they are operationally trustworthy. The gap between installed and truly ready can be expensive, especially in dense, power-constrained, or fast-tracked environments.

## Why this category matters

- commissioning validates whether systems actually perform under the intended operating model
- operations readiness determines whether the site can be run safely and predictably after handoff
- modular projects often compress schedules, which increases the risk of shallow turnover if readiness work is weak
- buyers need to compare not just test scripts but the maturity of the turnover and operating model itself

## Where it fits in a modular deployment

- follows installation and integration as systems near live operation
- supports handoff across electrical, cooling, controls, monitoring, and operational teams
- helps reduce risk in phased go-live sequences and rapid capacity activation
- becomes increasingly important when the environment includes advanced cooling, generation, or dense AI infrastructure

## What to compare

- depth of systems testing, validation, and issue-closeout discipline
- operational handoff quality, documentation, and runbook readiness
- experience with modular, high-density, or mission-critical environments
- ability to support phased activation and live-facility constraints
- connection between commissioning outcomes and ongoing operations workflows

## Typical buyer questions

- Are we validating performance under real operating conditions or only checklist conditions?
- How ready is the operations team to run this environment after handoff?
- What hidden risks usually appear late in turnover?
- Can this approach support phased live-up without creating rework?
- How much of the commissioning work actually improves long-term reliability?
