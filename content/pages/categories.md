# Category Graph

The category graph is the structural core of modulardatacenters.ai, designed to expand across every major segment involved in modern data center sourcing, deployment, and operations.

This is not a narrow parts directory. It is a wide market map spanning modular data centers, AI data centers, bitcoin infrastructure, colo, rental, power, cooling, software, engineering, land, logistics, and the systems around them.

## Featured categories

- Facility formats including modular, prefab, containerized, edge, colo, rental, and specialized deployment models
- Mechanical and thermal systems including liquid cooling, immersion, HVAC, heat rejection, airflow, and environmental control
- Electrical and energy systems including UPS, switchgear, generators, transformers, busway, batteries, and microgrids
- Software, controls, commissioning, logistics, construction, land, permitting, operations, and adjacent infrastructure services

## What this section means

- Each category is a node in a larger ecosystem, not just a flat directory bucket.
- The design should support continuous expansion into new adjacent segments without a full information-architecture rewrite.
- Internal linking between categories, vendors, guides, and compare pages is part of the product, not an afterthought.
- The goal is to become the largest browsable resource for data center infrastructure discovery with a primary focus on modular and adjacent markets.
